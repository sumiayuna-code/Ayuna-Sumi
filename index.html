Biography

Born and raised in Japan, Ayuna Sumi is a clarinetist pursuing a Master's degree in Clarinet Performance at the University of North Texas. She serves as co-principal clarinet with the University of North Texas Symphony Orchestra, where her playing is noted for its precision, musicality, and expressive depth.

Ayuna has extensive ensemble experience, having performed with the Fort Worth Symphony Orchestra, the National Orchestra Institute (2025), and the Dallas Winds wind orchestra. Her accolades include First Place in the Pi Kappa Lambda Scholarship (2025), 1st Prize in the George Papich Chamber Music Competition (2025), and she was a Finalist in the International Clarinet Association Young Artist Competition (2025).

She earned her Bachelor's degree at Hiroshima University and studied privately with Brooks Nobuo Thon. Currently, she studies with Kimberly Cole Luevano and Stanislav Chernyshev at the University of North Texas.
